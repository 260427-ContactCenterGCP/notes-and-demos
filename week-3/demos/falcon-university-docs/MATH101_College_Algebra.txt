MATH101 — College Algebra

Course Name: College Algebra

Course Number: MATH101

Credits: 3

Department: Mathematics

Professor: Dr. Laura Bennett

Semester Availability:
Fall, Spring, Summer

Course Description:
This course covers algebraic concepts and mathematical reasoning needed for higher-level mathematics and technical disciplines. Students study equations, functions, and graphing techniques.

Topics Covered:

equations
functions
graphing

Prerequisites:
None

Recommended Background:
High school algebra.

Difficulty Level:
Beginner

Estimated Weekly Workload:
5–7 hours

Student Feedback:
Students appreciate the clear structure and step-by-step examples. Graphing functions is commonly identified as the most important skill learned.

Career Relevance:
Provides foundational mathematics knowledge required for STEM, business, and technical programs.

Related Courses:

MATH210 Discrete Mathematics
STAT200 Introduction to Statistics

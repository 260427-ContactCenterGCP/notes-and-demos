CS301 — Algorithms

Course Name: Algorithms

Course Number: CS301

Credits: 4

Department: Computer Science

Professor: Dr. Priya Natarajan

Semester Availability:
Fall

Course Description:
This course focuses on the design, analysis, and optimization of algorithms. Students study algorithm efficiency, recursion techniques, sorting and searching algorithms, and introductory dynamic programming concepts.

Topics Covered:

sorting
searching
recursion
dynamic programming

Prerequisites:

CS201 Data Structures
MATH210 Discrete Mathematics

Recommended Background:
Strong programming skills and familiarity with mathematical logic.

Difficulty Level:
Advanced

Estimated Weekly Workload:
10–12 hours

Student Feedback:
Students describe this course as mathematically intensive and demanding. Many recommend practicing coding problems regularly outside of class. Dynamic programming is often considered the toughest topic.

Career Relevance:
Essential preparation for technical interviews, software engineering roles, machine learning, and advanced computer science coursework.

Related Courses:

CS410 Artificial Intelligence Fundamentals
MATH210 Discrete Mathematics

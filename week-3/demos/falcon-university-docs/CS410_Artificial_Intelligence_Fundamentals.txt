CS410 — Artificial Intelligence Fundamentals

Course Name: Artificial Intelligence Fundamentals

Course Number: CS410

Credits: 4

Department: Computer Science

Professor: Dr. Rebecca Lin

Semester Availability:
Spring

Course Description:
This course introduces the foundational concepts of artificial intelligence and machine learning. Students explore neural networks, natural language processing, ethical concerns in AI systems, and practical applications of intelligent technologies.

Topics Covered:

machine learning
neural networks
NLP
AI ethics

Prerequisites:

CS301 Algorithms
STAT200 Introduction to Statistics

Recommended Background:
Strong programming skills and familiarity with probability and linear algebra.

Difficulty Level:
Advanced

Estimated Weekly Workload:
10–14 hours

Student Feedback:
Students find the material exciting and highly relevant to current technology trends. Many report that machine learning concepts require significant independent study and practice.

Career Relevance:
Supports careers in artificial intelligence, machine learning engineering, data science, and research.

Related Courses:

MATH210 — Discrete Mathematics

Course Name: Discrete Mathematics

Course Number: MATH210

Credits: 3

Department: Mathematics

Professor: Dr. Kevin Marshall

Semester Availability:
Fall, Spring

Course Description:
This course introduces mathematical structures commonly used in computer science. Students study logic, proofs, sets, and graph theory with an emphasis on problem-solving and analytical reasoning.

Topics Covered:

logic
proofs
sets
graph theory

Prerequisites:
MATH101 College Algebra

Recommended Background:
Recommended for Computer Science majors.

Difficulty Level:
Intermediate

Estimated Weekly Workload:
7–9 hours

Student Feedback:
Students often find proof-writing difficult at first but rewarding after practice. Many say the course improves their logical thinking and programming skills.

Career Relevance:
Essential preparation for algorithms, cybersecurity, software engineering, and theoretical computer science.

Related Courses:

CS301 Algorithms
CS201 Data Structures

STAT200 — Introduction to Statistics

Course Name: Introduction to Statistics

Course Number: STAT200

Credits: 3

Department: Statistics

Professor: Dr. Natalie Brooks

Semester Availability:
Fall, Spring, Summer

Course Description:
This course introduces statistical reasoning and data analysis techniques. Students learn probability concepts, statistical distributions, regression analysis, and hypothesis testing methods.

Topics Covered:

probability
distributions
regression
hypothesis testing

Prerequisites:
MATH101 College Algebra

Recommended Background:
Basic algebra skills.

Difficulty Level:
Intermediate

Estimated Weekly Workload:
6–8 hours

Student Feedback:
Students value the practical applications of statistics and data interpretation. Hypothesis testing is frequently described as the most challenging topic.

Career Relevance:
Useful for careers in data science, research, business analytics, healthcare, and artificial intelligence.

Related Courses:

CS410 Artificial Intelligence Fundamentals
MATH210 Discrete Mathematics
